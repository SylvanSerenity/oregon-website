Did you know?

  • The town of Oswego was founded in 1847 and named after Oswego, New York. It was later renamed Lake Oswego in 1970 after Oswego Lake. 3
  • Oswego Lake has been the topic of multiple lawsuits due to the city restricting public access to the lake, which is almost entirely surrounded by private housing. In 2019, the Oregon Supreme Court ruled that the public had the right to access lake. 3
  • Lake Oswego has the highest median household income of $127,252 as of 2022. 2
  • Because of its high income status, racially-exclusive history, and the city being a predominantly white suburn area, it has been given nicknames such as "Fake Oswego", "Lake Big Ego", and "Lake No-Negro". A Lakeridge High School student produced the documentary, Lake No Negro, in 2020, discussing the city's history of racial exlusivity. 3
  • Many NFL, NBA, and MLB players have lived in Lake Oswego, including LaMarcus Aldridge, Jon Arnett, Terry Dischinger, and Michael Stutes. The lead singer of Everclear, Art Alexakis, also lived in Lake Oswego. 3
